The Cultural Context

Understanding the significance of Ethiopian food traditions

Communal Dining

Ethiopian cuisine is traditionally served on a large shared platter called a "gebeta," with injera flatbread as both plate and utensil. Diners sit around the gebeta and use pieces of injera to scoop up the various stews and dishes.

Fasting Traditions

Many Ethiopians observe Orthodox Christian fasting days, abstaining from animal products for over 200 days a year. This has led to a rich tradition of vegan cuisine that features prominently in Ethiopian cooking.
